# Landfall v1.3, Phase 1 result — a 4th historical storm (Mangkhut/Ompong, 2018)

## Why this phase exists, and why this storm

PRD §2's original v1 non-goal ("no nationwide coverage — only regions affected by the
three replay storms") was a deliberate boundary, not an oversight. Adding a 4th storm
was explicit author direction, not something suggested unprompted — the same
verification discipline applies, one storm at a time, not a drift toward nationwide
coverage.

Two candidates were checked before picking one, not assumed: **Mangkhut/Ompong (2018,
Northern Luzon)** and **Bopha/Pablo (2012, Mindanao→Visayas)**. Both IBTrACS SIDs were
fetched and their resolved names verified directly (not guessed from a constructed SID
pattern — an initial guess for both was wrong and had to be corrected by searching
IBTrACS by name instead). Bopha's actual PH-transit track (lat 7.5–12°, lon 116.5–127°,
read from the real per-timestep data) substantially overlaps **both** Haiyan's and
Odette's existing ROIs — reopening the multi-storm ROI-overlap complexity from v1.1
Phase 3. Mangkhut's corridor (Northern Luzon, lat ~14.5–19.5°) overlaps none of the
other three. Mangkhut was chosen for the clean ROI alone, verified before deciding, not
retrofitted after.

## Data verified, not guessed

- **IBTrACS**: SID `2018250N12170`, fetched and confirmed to resolve to name
  `MANGKHUT` via `get_track()`'s own name-match assertion.
- **PH-local name**: "Ompong" — confirmed via two independent sources found live (an
  HDX dataset titled "Typhoon Mangkhut (Ompong)" and ReliefWeb's own sitrep title),
  not recalled from memory alone.
- **ROI bounds** `(119.0, 15.5, 123.5, 19.5)`: derived from the actual track's
  PH-transit segment (2018-09-14 to 2018-09-15, lat/lon read directly from the fetched
  data), covering Cagayan/Isabela (landfall near Baggao, ~18.06°N 121.87°E) west
  through Ilocos/Cordillera to the exit into the West Philippine Sea.
- **Sitrep**: NDRRMC Update SitRep No. 50 (as of 30 September 2018, 6:00 AM), found on
  ReliefWeb (`data/sitreps/mangkhut/sitrep_50.pdf`) — `ndrrmc.gov.ph` itself blocks
  automated requests, same as for the original three; ReliefWeb mirrors it, same
  sourcing method as `docs/phase4b-result.md` already established.
- **E1 ground truth**: the sitrep's own "COST OF DAMAGES" table gives a clean combined
  Infrastructure + Agriculture total — ₱33,692,891,286.73 — the same combined-total
  structure already used for Rolly's E1 figure. Converted at the World Bank's 2018
  annual-average rate (₱52.6614/USD, fetched directly, not reused from Rolly's 2020
  rate) to **$639,802,058.47**.

## Wind field sanity check, not just a number

Before trusting the pipeline's output, the simulated field was checked against a known
physical landmark: **Itogon, Benguet**, where a rain-triggered landslide killed ~100
people (DOH-CAR), was the storm's single deadliest incident. The nearest simulated
centroid showed **0 m/s** — initially concerning enough to check whether it was an
ROI-boundary artifact rather than a real result. A full north-south wind transect at
Itogon's longitude shows a smooth, continuous field (genuinely 0 kn from lat 15.5–16.83,
ramping smoothly through 17.5 m/s at 16.875°, peaking ~101 kn near Baggao's landfall
latitude, decaying smoothly north) — not a cliff or edge artifact. **Itogon's near-zero
simulated wind is a real model output**, and it's a clean, independent illustration of
the wind-only model's known blind spot: this storm's deadliest event was rain/geohazard-
driven, not wind-driven, exactly the same category of gap already flagged for Rolly's
flood share (`docs/v1.2-phase1-result.md` and the README's E2 section).

## E1 result

| Storm | Point estimate | Interquartile range | NDRRMC actual | Error factor (point) | Bracketed? |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Mangkhut (2018) | $125,983,187.57 | $7,589,941.04 – $931,567,868.90 | $639,802,058.47 | **5.08× under** | **✓** |

The actual figure falls inside the interquartile range, consistent with the v1.2
finding holding for a storm the range-calibration approach has never seen before — a
real out-of-sample check, not just a fourth data point confirming what three storms
already showed. 5.08× under sits in the same rough band as the other three storms'
final-state error factors (Haiyan 1.18–1.32× under, Rolly 2.54–2.86× under, Odette
3.4–7.7× over) — another storm the single national curve doesn't fit exceptionally
well or exceptionally badly, consistent with v1.1 Phase 5's finding that no single
calibration fits all of these storms precisely.

Geographic sanity check: the top-damage municipality is **Laoag City, Ilocos Norte**
($89.8M) — sitting almost exactly on the track's actual exit path (the storm passed
directly over the Ilocos Norte coast at ~18.2°N, 120.1°E on its way out to the West
Philippine Sea, per the fetched track), independently consistent with real-world
reporting that Ilocos Norte was among the hardest-hit provinces.

## A second real compiler bug, found the same way as the first (E3)

Testing the new storm's name handling the same way E3's earlier storm-name round did
(`docs/phase7-result.md` / the E3 README section) found a second real fuzzy-matching
bug: **"Typhoon Mangkut" (missing the "h") was silently accepted as Mangkhut**, stable
across repeated calls. An explicit contrastive prompt example (matching the pattern that
fixed "Yolande" and "Ray") was added, but **this one did not resolve** — 4/4 calls still
wrongly accept it after the fix. This is harder and more stable than the flaky "Typhoon
Rai" residual miss; per the project's established practice, further prompt-tuning
against one fixed case (given the earlier oscillation risk: fixing Yolande broke Rai/
Goni, fixing those reopened Ray, fixing that broke STY-prefix parsing) was not
attempted further. Disclosed rather than hidden: added to `evals/e3_dataset.json` as a
known-failing invalid case (id 85) rather than excluded to protect the accuracy number.
E3 with this case included: **48/48 exact-config (100%), 36/37 rejection (97.3%)** — no
regressions on any of the previously-fixed cases (Yolande, Ray, Rai, Goni, STY-prefix
all still pass).

## A real bug found by adding a 4th storm: duplicated, drifting state

`landfall narrate mangkhut` crashed outright: `cli.py`'s `cmd_narrate` had a **hardcoded
`{"haiyan": 2013, "rolly": 2020, "odette": 2021}` year lookup** with no entry for the
new storm — a `KeyError`, not a graceful failure. The same hardcoded dict, verbatim,
was independently duplicated in `evals/e2_groundedness.py` (twice). This is exactly the
kind of scattered, silently-drifting state a new storm was always going to stress-test.
Fixed at the root, not per call site: added `year: int` to `StormConfig` itself
(verified against each storm's actual fetched track timestamps, not guessed — including
double-checking the original three, not just trusting they were already right), and
updated all three call sites to read `STORMS[key].year` instead of maintaining a
parallel mapping that can go stale. `evals/e2_groundedness.py` now automatically scales
to whatever's in the `STORMS` registry (63 briefings at three storms → 84 at four),
not hardcoded to "three" anywhere.

## What this phase doesn't resolve

- The compiler's "Mangkut" misspelling (above) — a known, disclosed, currently-failing
  edge case, not silently excluded from the eval.
- No flood-share or wind-vs-rain attribution investigation was attempted for
  Mangkhut's own Itogon landslide, matching the same "not answerable from sector-based
  sitreps" limitation already found for Rolly (`docs/v1.2-phase1-result.md`) rather than
  re-litigated here.
- RAG corpus grounding for Mangkhut's sitrep — table-aware extraction and embedding
  were rerun to include it, verified separately (see commit).
- Whether other hardcoded per-storm dicts exist elsewhere in the codebase beyond the
  two found here was not exhaustively re-audited beyond a direct grep for this exact
  pattern.

## What to verify manually before committing
