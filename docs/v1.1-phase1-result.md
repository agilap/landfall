# Landfall v1.1, Phase 1 result — vulnerability curve recalibration

## Why this phase exists

E1 (the v1 baseline) showed Haiyan underestimated 18.6× and Rolly 575×, with Odette
alone landing in the PRD's stated 2–5× range for typical TC-model error. Working
hypothesis going in: the impact function's vulnerability curve was mismatched to
Philippine construction.

## What was actually wrong

The premise needed correcting before fixing anything. `landfall/impact/engine.py`
already used CLIMADA's WP2 region — which *is* the Philippines
(`CountryCode.ALPHA3["WP2"] == ["PHL"]`, `CountryCode.REGION_NAME["WP2"] ==
"Philippines"`) — not a generic/mismatched curve. The real bug was one config value:
`calibration_approach="TDR"`.

Eberenz et al. 2021 (NHESS 21:393–415, https://doi.org/10.5194/nhess-21-393-2021)
publish three calibrations of the same WP2 curve's `v_half` (wind speed at 50% damage,
the only regionally-varying shape parameter — `v_thresh=25.7 m/s` is fixed per Emanuel
2011 regardless of region):

| Approach | v_half (WP2) | What it optimizes |
|---|---|---|
| TDR (v1's choice, CLIMADA's default) | 188.4 m/s | total simulated damage = total reported damage, summed across all matched regional events |
| **RMSF (this phase's choice)** | **84.7 m/s** | equal weight per matched event (log-scale), insensitive to any single event's damage magnitude |
| EDR | 87.6 m/s | median of a v_half fitted independently to each matched event |

Fetched the paper directly rather than relying on recall. It explicitly names this as a
known WP2–4 problem: *"TDR gives larger weight to events with large damage values...
these results indicate that these events are systematically overestimated by the model
in the regions WP2–4"* — producing an anomalously flat, high-v_half curve. Philippines
is one of the regions the paper names. `docs/impact_curves_tdr_vs_rmsf.png` (generated
by `scripts/plot_impact_curves.py`, reading `v_half` live from CLIMADA's bundled
calibration files rather than hardcoding it) shows the shape difference: at Haiyan's
peak wind speed (~87 m/s), TDR's curve is at ~5% mean damage degree; RMSF's is at ~53%.

**Change made:** `engine.py`'s `calibration_approach="TDR"` → `IMPF_CALIBRATION_APPROACH
= "RMSF"`. No new impact function was written, no parameter was invented — this
switches between two already-published calibrations of the same published curve.
RMSF over EDR because it's the metric Eberenz et al. designed specifically to avoid the
large-event-domination bias just described; EDR's median-of-per-event-fits (84.7 vs 87.6
m/s) lands close enough to cross-validate the direction of the fix either way. Scenario
cache (`data/cache/scenarios/`) cleared — its hash key doesn't include the calibration
approach, so old TDR-computed results would otherwise be served unchanged.

## E1 waterfall

| Storm | Baseline (TDR) | Fix 1: RMSF calibration | NDRRMC actual |
|---|---|---|---|
| Haiyan (2013) | $49.3M (18.6× under) | $775.6M (**1.18× under**) | ~$917M |
| Rolly (2020) | $0.40M (575× under) | $7.86M (**29.6× under**) | ~$233M |
| Odette (2021) — **held out** | $184.1M (2.5–5.0× under) | $3,532.1M (**3.9–7.7× over**) | $459M–$915M |

Fit on Haiyan + Rolly only, per the working rule. Odette was not touched, previewed, or
used to pick RMSF over EDR/TDR — its number above is the curve's result on a storm the
calibration choice never saw.

## The Odette finding — reported prominently, not minimized

**RMSF calibration flips Odette from underestimation to overestimation: 2.5–5.0× under
becomes 3.9–7.7× over.** This is the central result of this phase, not a footnote. The
same single-region curve that closes most of Haiyan's gap and a third of Rolly's log-gap
overshoots Odette by a comparable margin in the other direction. That is not what a
correctly-calibrated regional curve should do to a held-out storm from the *same*
region, and it should not be read as "two out of three storms improved" — a curve that
systematically overestimates the storm it wasn't tuned against is exactly as informative
as one that systematically underestimates it.

Two honest candidate explanations, neither investigated further here (out of Phase 1
scope — this phase is vulnerability-curve-only):
- Odette/Rai's damage was unusually concentrated in a few high-exposure areas (Metro
  Cebu — see `docs/phase5-result.md`'s municipality breakdown) in a way a single
  national-average `v_half` can't represent for both a spread-out storm (Haiyan, Rolly)
  and a concentrated one.
- Rolly's *own* residual 29.6× underestimation after the same fix suggests a second,
  independent problem (hazard field or exposure, not vulnerability curve) large enough
  that Odette's apparent "good v1 baseline" may have been two errors partially
  cancelling rather than one thing being correct.

Not resolved, not tuned around — flagged per the working rule that a fix explained away
by only looking at the storms it was fit on is overfitting.
