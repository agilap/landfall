# Landfall v1.1, Phase 5 result — why Odette flipped to overestimation

## The question

Phase 1 flipped Odette from 2.5–5.0× under to 3.9–7.7× over when the WP2 impact
function's calibration switched from `TDR` to `RMSF`, and flagged this as unexplained —
"concentrated-vs-spread damage profile is a hypothesis, not a confirmed cause." This
phase tests that hypothesis directly instead of leaving it as a hypothesis.

## First hypothesis (Metro Cebu concentration) — real, but not the actual mechanism

Odette's damage is genuinely dominated by Metro Cebu: Cebu City ($1.29B), Lapu-Lapu
City ($877M), and Mandaue City ($379M) together are 72.2% of the RMSF-era total
($3,532.1M). Simulated wind there is a moderate 49–58 m/s (Cebu City 52.1 m/s peak,
Lapu-Lapu 58.1 m/s) — well below Haiyan's ~87 m/s peak. At those specific wind speeds,
the RMSF/TDR mean-damage-degree ratio is unusually large: **19.3× at Cebu City's 52
m/s, 18.2× at Lapu-Lapu's 58 m/s**, both higher than the ratio at Haiyan's peak (10.4×
at 87 m/s) or Rolly's landfall (13.9× at 74 m/s). Recomputing Odette's total under both
curves split by Metro Cebu vs. everywhere else:

| | TDR | RMSF | multiplier |
|---|---|---|---|
| Metro Cebu (3 cities) | $133.75M (72.7% of total) | $2,550.5M (72.2% of total) | 19.07× |
| Everywhere else in Odette's ROI | $50.32M | $981.6M | 19.51× |

**Metro Cebu's share of the total barely moved (72.7%→72.2%).** If concentrated urban
exposure at moderate wind speed were the actual mechanism specific to Odette, Metro
Cebu's *share* of the swing should have grown relative to the rest of the ROI. It
didn't — both halves scaled by almost exactly the same factor. Metro Cebu is *where*
a lot of dollar value sits, not *why* the total overshot.

## The actual mechanism: a near-uniform multiplier applied to three very different starting deficits

Computed the exact TDR→RMSF multiplier for all three storms directly (not estimated):

| Storm | TDR total | RMSF total | Multiplier |
|---|---|---|---|
| Haiyan | $49,327,691.86 | $775,591,763.78 | **15.72×** |
| Rolly (RMSF alone, pre-hazard/exposure fixes) | $404,699.55 | $7,863,561.41 | **19.43×** |
| Odette | $184,074,084.28 | $3,532,051,319.38 | **19.19×** |

All three land in the same 15.7–19.4× band — the curve swap multiplies every storm's
total by roughly the same factor, because in each case most of that storm's
damage-weighted exposure sits somewhere in the sigmoid's sensitive middle range,
where RMSF's much lower v_half (84.7 vs. TDR's 188.4 m/s) produces a large relative MDD
increase regardless of the storm's specific wind-speed distribution. Applying that
near-uniform multiplier to each storm's actual pre-existing TDR-era deficit predicts
the post-RMSF error factor almost exactly:

- Haiyan: 18.6× under ÷ 15.72 → **1.183× under** (actual: 1.18× under)
- Rolly: 575× under ÷ 19.43 → **29.6× under** (actual: 29.6× under)
- Odette: 2.5–5.0× under ÷ 19.19 → **0.130–0.261**, i.e. **3.84–7.68× over** (actual: 3.9–7.7× over)

All three predictions match the observed post-RMSF error factors to within rounding.
**Odette didn't get a uniquely bad or storm-specific multiplier — it started closest to
correct under TDR (only 2.5–5× under, vs. Haiyan's 18.6× and Rolly's 575×), so the same
proportional correction that rescues the other two storms necessarily overshoots the
one that needed the least correcting.** This is arithmetic, not a bug or a
storm-specific data problem, and Metro Cebu's dominant share of Odette's total is
incidental to it, not the cause.

## The uncomfortable implication

This isn't an Odette-specific or Cebu-specific quirk fixable by more investigation of
Odette in isolation. It's a structural property of replacing one single national
`v_half` with another: **no single v_half closes an 18.6×, a 575×, and a 2.5–5×
deficit simultaneously without over- or under-shooting at least one of them.** A curve
calibrated to correct the worst-case deficit (Rolly) would overshoot Haiyan and Odette
by even more; a curve calibrated to leave Odette untouched would leave Haiyan and Rolly
far more underestimated than RMSF does. Phase 1's choice of RMSF over TDR was
well-justified by the source paper's own stated purpose (avoiding large-event
domination bias) and by Haiyan/Rolly's results — but it was never going to produce one
number that's simultaneously right for all three storms, because their starting
deficits were too far apart for any single-parameter linear-in-log-space fix to
reconcile.

## What this changes going forward

- This is not "unexplained" anymore — it's resolved, and the explanation is
  structural, not a specific bug to patch.
- It reframes what a future fix would need to look like: not a different single
  national `v_half`, but something that can vary by more than "one number per WP
  region" — e.g. exposure-density-dependent calibration, a different functional form
  entirely, or accepting that a single wind-only impact function has an irreducible
  spread across storms this different and reporting a range rather than a point
  estimate per storm.
- Does not change any number in the waterfall — this phase only explains the Phase 1
  result, it doesn't re-run or adjust anything.
