# Landfall v1.1, Phase 3 result — exposure upgrade for the Rolly-affected region

## Why this phase exists

Phases 1–2 closed Haiyan's gap and flagged, without closing, Rolly's remaining 29.6×
underestimation. Working hypothesis: LitPop distributes national produced-capital value
by population × nightlights (Eberenz et al. 2020), which structurally undervalues
low-luminosity rural areas — precisely Catanduanes and rural Bicol. This phase replaces
LitPop with OSM building footprints + PSA 2020 census population for the three
Rolly-affected provinces only (Catanduanes, Albay, Camarines Sur); everywhere else in
every storm's ROI stays LitPop, untouched.

## Design (task 1)

Current exposure (`landfall/exposure/litpop.py`): `LitPop.from_countries("PHL",
res_arcsec=150, fin_mode="pc")` disaggregates a national produced-capital total across
a 150-arcsec grid weighted by GPW gridded population and NASA Black Marble nightlights,
then clips to ROI. `fin_mode="pop"` does the same for population count.

New layer (`landfall/exposure/hybrid.py`): one exposure point per real OSM building,
valued at `footprint_area_m2 × building:levels (default 1) × PHP_per_sqm / PHP_per_USD`;
one exposure point per barangay centroid, valued at its actual 2020 census population.
Both replace LitPop only inside the three provinces' real GADM boundaries (not just a
bounding box, to avoid double-counting at the edge against LitPop everywhere else).

## Data sources and vintages (task 2)

- **OSM building footprints** — live Overpass API query (feasibility confirmed
  live before building anything), extracted 2026-07-05: **176,513 buildings (Albay),
  85,474 (Camarines Sur), 61,022 (Catanduanes)**. This is a rolling present-day OSM
  snapshot, not a fixed 2020 vintage — a real, disclosed temporal mismatch against
  Rolly's landfall (buildings built or demolished 2020–2026 aren't reflected as they
  stood at landfall time). `building:levels` tag coverage is essentially absent (8 of
  4,588 buildings checked in a Bato, Catanduanes spot-check — 0.17%), so floor area is,
  in practice, footprint area for nearly every building, not true multi-story floor
  area — a real simplification, not silently assumed away.
- **Population**: PSA 2020 Census of Population and Housing, barangay level, via HDX
  (`data.humdata.org`) since `psa.gov.ph` itself returns HTTP 403 from this
  environment (confirmed, not assumed — same block affects the construction-cost
  source below). **Same year as Rolly — no temporal mismatch on this input.**
- **Barangay boundaries**: GADM v4.1 ADM_ADM_3 (already cached from Phase 5), name-
  joined to the PSA population table. **Barangay-level exact-name match rate: 80.0%
  (1,681/2,100)** across the three provinces — the remainder get population via an
  area-proportional split of their municipality's *unaccounted* population (not a
  province-wide average — computed per-municipality so each municipality's true PSA
  total is conserved exactly). Two real name-matching bugs were caught and fixed before
  finalizing this, not left as silent undercounts: PSA appends parenthetical alt-names
  to some municipalities ("Virac (Capital)", "San Andres (Calolbon)") that GADM's plain
  names don't carry, and PSA/GADM disagree on city name word order ("City of Naga" vs
  "Naga City") and on diacritics ("Sagñay" vs "Sagnay"). Before these fixes, Albay and
  Camarines Sur's computed provincial totals were undercounted by 468,590 and 360,468
  people respectively (an entire major municipality's population silently dropped per
  province) — caught by checking the computed total against PSA's own reported
  provincial total, not assumed correct. **After fixing: all three provinces' computed
  totals match PSA's reported totals exactly (diff = 0).**
- **Construction cost rate**: PHP 9,949/m², a commercial quantity-surveyor industry
  estimate (national average, single-family residential) — **not** PSA's own
  region-specific "Approved Building Permits" statistics, which are also blocked
  (`psa.gov.ph`, 403, same as above). Applied uniformly to all three provinces since no
  Bicol-specific figure could be independently confirmed; this was an explicit,
  disclosed trade-off decision, not a default fallen into.
- **Exchange rate**: PHP 49.624/USD, World Bank WDI series `PA.NUS.FCRF`, 2020 annual
  average — fetched directly from the World Bank API, not invented.

## Sanity checks (task 3) — reported even where awkward

**Total exposed asset value, Catanduanes, LitPop vs new layer**:

| Layer | Total value | Per-capita (÷271,879 2020 census pop.) |
|---|---|---|
| LitPop (68 grid points) | $27,876,403 | $102.53 |
| OSM/hybrid (61,022 buildings) | $774,683,222 | $2,849.37 |

**27.79× higher.** Checked this wasn't a units bug before treating it as a finding:
LitPop's implied $103/person is implausible on its face (less than the cost of a bag of
cement); the new layer's ~$2,850/person is a plausible order of magnitude against
Philippine per-capita GDP (~$3,300 in 2020) for a province's total building stock. The
size of the gap is itself evidence of how severe LitPop's rural undervaluation is here,
not a red flag that something broke.

**OSM building counts vs PSA occupied-housing-unit counts** (PSA Census 2020, Housing
Characteristics by Construction Materials — a separate HDX-hosted table, fetched
specifically to check this):

| Province | OSM buildings | PSA occupied housing units | OSM / PSA |
|---|---|---|---|
| Catanduanes | 61,022 | 59,815 | **102.0%** |
| Albay | 176,513 | 305,362 | **57.8%** |
| Camarines Sur | 85,474 | 445,150 | **19.2%** |

This is the single most important honest finding of this phase. **OSM mapping
completeness is wildly uneven across the three provinces** — Catanduanes is
essentially fully mapped (buildings slightly exceed housing units, consistent with
non-residential structures also being counted), but Albay captures barely over half of
its actual housing stock, and Camarines Sur — the most populous of the three, with more
housing units than the other two combined — captures under a fifth. The new exposure
layer almost certainly **undercounts** true building stock in Albay and especially
Camarines Sur. Framed against E1 below: the improvement reported there was achieved
*despite* this undercount, which if anything suggests the correction understates what a
complete building dataset would show, rather than overselling it.

## E1 waterfall (task 4)

Phase 1 curve (RMSF) and Phase 2 hazard configuration (no change adopted) held fixed;
only the exposure layer changed, and only for the three target provinces.

| Storm | Baseline (TDR) | Fix 1: RMSF curve | Fix 2: hazard (Phase 2) | Fix 3: hybrid exposure (Phase 3) | NDRRMC actual |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Haiyan (2013) | $49.3M (18.6× under) | $775.6M (1.18× under) | no change | **$775.6M (unchanged)** | ~$917M |
| Rolly (2020) | $0.40M (575× under) | $7.86M (29.6× under) | no change | **$91.6M (2.54× under)** | ~$233M |
| Odette (2021) — held out | $184.1M (2.5–5.0× under) | $3,532.1M (3.9–7.7× over) | no change | **$3,532.1M (unchanged)** | $459M–$915M |

Haiyan's and Odette's totals are bit-for-bit identical to Phase 1/2, confirmed rather
than assumed: Odette's ROI has zero geometric overlap with the three target provinces
(checked directly), and Haiyan's ROI has a small sliver overlapping Albay's southern
edge, but Haiyan's wind field there is far from the storm's actual track (Leyte/Samar/
Cebu) and contributes zero damage regardless of which exposure layer sits under it —
also confirmed by the identical total, not just expected.

## How much of Rolly's gap this closes (task 5)

**A lot — this is not a "little" finding.** Rolly moved from **29.6× under to 2.54×
under**, roughly an 11.7× reduction in the damage error, and for the first time lands
*inside* the PRD's own stated 2–5× range for typical TC-model error (the same range
Odette sat in before Phase 1, and no longer sits in after it). The exposure layer, not
the vulnerability curve or hazard configuration, was the dominant remaining error
source for Rolly.

Virac (Catanduanes' capital, also its highest-simulated-wind municipality per Phase 2)
is now the top-damage municipality at $49.8M, followed by Buhi (Camarines Sur, $11.1M)
and Baras (Catanduanes, $7.7M) — a sensible ranking now that exposure reflects where
people and buildings actually are. Bato — Phase 2's flagged anomaly, showing only 26.5
m/s simulated wind against neighboring municipalities' 70+ m/s — is still not
prominent, because this phase didn't touch the hazard field; that anomaly remains
open.

## What this doesn't resolve

- **The OSM undercount in Albay/Camarines Sur (above) means Rolly's true damage,
  under a complete building dataset, is plausibly higher than $91.6M** — the 2.54×
  under figure may itself still be an underestimate of how good this fix really is,
  or the gap could close further with better OSM coverage. Not resolved here.
- **The construction-cost rate is a national commercial estimate, not a Bicol-specific
  PSA figure** — if Bicol's real regional rate differs meaningfully from the national
  ₱9,949/m² average (plausible in either direction), total value scales linearly with
  it.
- **`building:levels` is essentially unavailable** — floor area is, in practice,
  footprint area. Any real multi-story building is undervalued proportionally to its
  actual story count.
- **The OSM data is a 2026 snapshot, not 2020** — construction/demolition since Rolly
  is not reflected.
- **Phase 2's Bato anomaly is unresolved and orthogonal to this phase** — a hazard-side
  question, not something a better exposure layer touches.

## What to verify manually before committing

- The 27.79× Catanduanes value jump and the OSM/PSA housing-unit ratios above — these
  are the two most consequential numbers in this phase; worth an independent spot-check
  against your own knowledge of Bicol before trusting them fully.
- Whether the ₱9,949/m² national rate (rather than holding out for a Bicol-specific
  PSA figure) is an acceptable trade-off to have built on, given how much it drives the
  final USD value.
- Whether "2.54× under, but built on a building dataset confirmed only ~19–58% complete
  in two of three provinces" is a result you're comfortable reporting as a win, or
  whether the OSM completeness gap should be investigated (e.g. supplementing with the
  HDX "PH Open Buildings" dataset mentioned in Phase 3's design search, not used here)
  before treating this as closed.
